House of the Dragon: Release Date, Trailer, Cast, and Latest Updates

House Targaryen’s narrative will be shared. The precursor to Game of Thrones, House of the Dragon, is coming to HBO and HBO Max. So far, here’s everything you need to know. HBO’s House of Dragon, the Game of Thrones prequel series under development for television and HBO Max, is based on George R.R. Martin’s 2018 novel Fire & Blood. It looks to take viewers back to Westeros over two years after the original series ended—and hundreds of years before the events depicted.

House of Dragon will be directed by Ryan Condal (Colony, Hercules) and Miguel Sapochnik, with Game of Thrones writers David Benioff and D. B. Weiss off on their $250 million Netflix deal, working on another adaptation, this time sci-fi. Many Game of Thrones fans may remember the latter’s name from his directing credits. Benioff and Weiss frequently enlisted Sapochnik as a director for the series’ major battle episodes, like “Battle of the Bastards” and “The Long Night.” His dedication to the series should bode well for the future.
